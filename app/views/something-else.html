You may need to report the incident to your local authority or a utility company

Incidents you should report to local councils or utility companies

Find online guidance on how to report the following issues:

  • burst water mains
  • dangerous buildings or structures
  • discarded syringes
  • flooding from a brook or stream, or from any river that’s not a main river
  • fly-tipping
  • a litter problem
  • noise
  • pest nuisances

Incidents you should report to the Environment Agency

Call the Environment Agency to report the following:

  • collapsed or badly damaged river banks
  • damage or danger to the natural environment
  • dead fish or fish gasping for air
  • flooding from main rivers or the sea
  • people taking water illegally from rivers, streams, canals or underground sources such as wells
  • incidents at Environment Agency-regulated waste sites
  • main rivers blocked by a vehicle or fallen tree causing risk of flooding
  • poaching or illegal fishing
  • pollution to water or land
  • unusual changes in river flow
  • Environment Agency incident hotline
  • Telephone: 020 7946 0101
  • 24 hours
